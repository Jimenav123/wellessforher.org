Matcha vs Coffee?? What's better for someone with PCOS?

There is much debate over whether coffee or matcha is better for women struggling with PCOS, and it can get overwhelming and confusing. So, I am here to help simplify this. Matcha and coffee both have many benefits, including their high antioxidant content, which can help counteract the harmful effects of oxidative stress in your body. However, if you have PCOS it can be a completely different story.

Coffee and PCOS

Many people with PCOS also struggle with high cortisol levels, and coffee consumption can exacerbate this by increasing stress in the body. While coffee provides a quick energy boost, which most of us crave in the mornings or during long workdays, this rapid absorption can have drawbacks. Your body absorbs 99% of the caffeine from coffee, reaching peak blood concentration within 15 minutes of your first sip, providing long-lasting energy. Here are some pros and cons that coffee has for those dealing with PCOS:

  • Rapid Energy Boost: Pros: Coffee is quickly absorbed, providing an instant energy boost that can help during mornings or long workdays. Cons: The rapid absorption may lead to spikes in energy and afternoon crashes, which can affect mood and productivity. To avoid this crash, don't drink coffee within 60-90 minutes of waking and you will see an improvement in your energy levels throughout the day.
  • Impact on Hormone Levels: Pros: Coffee can temporarily increase alertness and concentration. Cons: Coffee can increase both cortisol and androgen levels, complicating hormone balance, something detrimental to PCOS.
  • Effects on Sleep: Pros: The initial boost can be beneficial for short-term energy needs. Cons: Coffee's half-life of up to 10 hours can disrupt sleep, exacerbating PCOS symptoms like fatigue.
  • Blood Sugar and Digestion: Pros: Black coffee has low calories, which can be beneficial for weight management. Cons: Coffee can spike blood sugar levels, especially with additives, and its high acidity can irritate the stomach lining, leading to digestive discomfort.

Matcha and PCOS

On the other hand, matcha is known for its numerous benefits, particularly for those dealing with hormonal imbalances like PCOS. Here’s why many PCOS specialists recommend green tea/matcha:

  • Improved Insulin Sensitivity: Studies show that green tea can enhance insulin sensitivity, helping to stabilize blood sugar levels and reduce insulin resistance, common issues for many women with PCOS.
  • Antioxidant Properties: Matcha is rich in polyphenols, which are powerful antioxidants. These compounds help reduce inflammation and oxidative stress, which can be beneficial for managing PCOS symptoms.
  • Stress Reduction: Matcha contains L-theanine, an amino acid that promotes relaxation by calming the mind without causing drowsiness. It also helps decrease stress on the adrenal glands, which can be beneficial for women with PCOS who experience elevated stress levels.
  • Weight Management: Green tea and matcha contain high levels of catechins, which can aid in weight management by boosting metabolism and increasing fat oxidation. This makes it easier to lose weight, a common goal for those managing PCOS.
  • Hormonal Regulation: Consumption of green tea and matcha may help regulate hormones by lowering testosterone levels and alleviating PCOS symptoms like acne and excessive hair growth.

Conclusion

While both coffee and matcha have many health benefits, matcha has been shown to have more advantages for those with PCOS symptoms because of its ability to promote relaxation without disrupting sleep. However, everyone can have different responses to coffee, and it’s essential to pay attention to how your body reacts to it.

For me, and many women dealing with hormonal issues, cutting out coffee was beneficial because it tended to cause inflammation in my body. Once I was able to stabilize my hormones, I noticed that when I incorporated coffee again, I was able to enjoy it without the side effects it once gave me.
