8 Ways I Reduced My Cortisol in 6 Months

Stress was the main factor that caused my health to quickly decline. When I was working with my holistic doctor, I was able to take a Dutch test, which is a hormonal panel, to see where my hormone levels were at with my PCOS. Little did I know the main factor that caused my hormones to go out of wack was the amount of stress my body had.

When my doctor looked at my results, she was in shock by how high my levels of cortisol, a stress hormone, were. She mentioned to me that she had never seen levels that high in a patient, so I knew I had to get straight to work. Chronic stress can disrupt the body's hormonal balance, potentially heightening the PCOS symptoms. Elevated cortisol levels can interfere with the reproductive hormone system, which often leads to irregular menstrual cycles. So, I knew if I ever wanted to feel good again, I would have to adapt a new way of living.

Understanding Cortisol and Adrenals

For my science geeks, this is a quick overview of what happens in your body with cortisol and adrenals: if you want to get straight into how I fixed it then keep scrolling down.

Cortisol is a hormone produced by your two adrenal glands which are located above your kidneys. Your adrenal glands consist of two main parts: the outer layer of your adrenal glands called your adrenal cortex, and the inner part of the adrenal gland called the adrenal medulla. The adrenal cortex produces hormones called corticosteroids, including cortisol. And the adrenal medulla produces adrenaline, epinephrine, and norepinephrine, which, as you've probably heard, are part of the fight or flight response.

Cortisol is often called the stress hormone because its levels usually rise in response to stress, helping the body to mobilize energy and respond to a perceived threat. In prolonged levels of high cortisol, your body may develop symptoms such as anxiety, depression, weight gain, memory fog, etc.

My Journey to Stress Reduction

It's been 6 months since I have been working to reduce my cortisol and stress levels. Although I could not change the external factors that made me stressed, I was able to change the way I dealt with the stress. Now, I feel like a different person. I have learned how to deal with everything that life throws at me in a way that won't affect my health. Here is how I was able to do it:

The main thing I did was to practice vagal nerve exercises. The vagal nerve is responsible for regulating your heart rate, meaning it can speed up your heart rate in response to stress or danger versus slowing down in response to relaxation. It also promotes the release of digestive enzymes for your body and is in charge of regulating your breathing. It can play a role in your immune system, by reducing inflammation within your body. Basically, it helps regulate your response to your flight or fight mode.

Having said this, I started practicing the following exercises to help control my vagal nerve:

Yoga / Stretching

1. This helps your parasympathetic nervous system because it causes your heart rate to slow down, not to mention the breathing techniques in these practices can ground you to the present moment.

2. Vagal tone is a measure of how well your body's calming system, the parasympathetic nervous system, is working. When you boost your vagal tone, it helps your body relax more quickly after stress. Since your vagus nerve is linked to your vocal cords, these practices can activate it.

3. Wim Hof's breathing technique involves deep breaths, rapid rhythmic breathing, and breath retention to induce an altered state of consciousness. It activates the parasympathetic nervous system, promoting relaxation and countering stress. Individual responses may vary, and consulting with a healthcare professional, especially for those with pre-existing health conditions, is advisable.

Taking Cold Showers / Cold Plunges

4. I take cold plunges or showers to increase dopamine and reduce stress. This has greatly helped with my inflammation and decreased body fat. I aim to do this for 11 minutes per week. Hint: To increase dopamine, I like to take caffeine 60-120min before the cold exposure.

Listen to Therapeutic Music in the Morning

5. Ever since I started listening to classical music in the morning, like Beethoven, I have gotten out of bed more quickly and woken up faster. Studies have found a link between classical music and a reduction in depression, anxiety, and stress.

Filling My Mouth With Saliva to Submerge My Tongue

6. This helps reduce stress, because the vagus nerve is a part of the parasympathetic nervous system, and controls the muscles around the oral cavity.

Grounding

7. When the body has contact with the earth's natural ground like sand, grass, etc it has an electric charge that stabilizes your physiology which reduces inflammation, pain, and stress, improves blood flow, energy, sleep, and generates greater well-being.

Natural Sunlight as Soon as I Wake Up

8. This has made a huge impact on my health because it has helped me regulate my circadian rhythm, our body clock that regulates when we are alert or sleepy, which has hugely aided in my sleep. It also has improved my mood by stimulating serotonin.

Results and Transformation

When I put these practices into my life, it not only improved my heart rate variability, but it also helped regulate my heartbeat under stress. By practicing these techniques I was able to train my body back into a parasympathetic “rest and digest” mode, instead of the “flight or fight” mode. Even though I know this can be hard at times, I always feel better after taking time to ground myself and focus on the present moment. I would have never guessed that taking these simple steps into my daily practice would make a tremendous difference in my daily life.
