Is There a Perfect Morning Routine If You Have PCOS?

Is there such a thing as having the perfect morning? Well, it depends on who you ask. A person's lifestyle and habits can definitively change how they feel and ultimately who they become.

For people with PCOS, lowering cortisol is the main goal. So, how can we achieve this? How should we start? I argue that a perfect morning routine, one that focuses on hormone regulation and habit optimization, can be crucial for managing PCOS.

First things first: How do we create a morning routine centered around beneficial habits? Interestingly, during the first eight hours of your day, you have the greatest ability to tackle tasks that cause you the most resistance, meaning anything you dread doing should be done in the morning. Fortunately, this gives us an advantage in forming new habits. So, let's explore what the perfect morning routine looks like for someone who eats well and exercises but still struggles with their results and health.

A High-Cortisol, Stress-Inducing Morning

You wake up at 7 a.m. and grab your phone immediately to check emails, social media, and texts, hoping this will wake you up. Once done with your social media, you groggily head to the kitchen to make your favorite cup of coffee, complete with your preferred milk and sugar. Next, you go to the bathroom, using hormone-disrupting chemicals in products like toothpaste and facewash. Afterward, you gear up for a run or a HIIT class, thinking this will help you lose weight and improve your health. Finally, an hour or two later, you prepare yourself breakfast.

This routine is harmful, causing more stress to your body than it needs. Your morning coffee with sugar, creamer, and the oils in milk is detrimental. Consuming this on an empty stomach stresses your body further, potentially disrupting your hormones and causing indigestion and upset stomach due to coffee's acidity.

Many women believe they're benefiting from HIIT workouts, hoping to see results, but often, issues like cortisol imbalance contribute to PCOS symptoms. Running or doing HIIT first thing in the morning raises cortisol levels, which can lead to problems like facial swelling, inflammation, and hormone irregularities.

My Perfect Morning Routine

To address these issues, the key is to establish a ritual that allows time for self-care and nourishing your body with love. This approach has been transformative for me, improving my day, mental health, PCOS symptoms, inflammation, brain fog, and weight. My perfect morning routine includes:

  • Waking up at the same time every morning to regulate my circadian rhythm. 10 p.m. to 7 a.m. works best for me.
  • Drinking a glass of water immediately upon waking helped boost my energy significantly.
  • Spending 10-20 minutes outdoors to get sunlight in my eyes. This improves daytime energy and mood, and I use a red light therapy mask when natural sunlight isn't available.
  • Meditating and doing breathwork during this time, or grounding myself by standing on grass or sand. This reduces inflammation and stress and improves sleep.
  • Having a savory breakfast such as an omelet or breakfast sandwich, paired with green tea, matcha, or a London Fog, helps with digestion and reduces inflammation. A savory breakfast also helps balance glucose levels and sets the tone for a productive day, preventing afternoon crashes and sugar cravings.
  • Engaging in gentle movement after breakfast, such as yoga, mat pilates, or a morning walk, helps prevent glucose spikes.

This morning routine has been immensely beneficial for me. Even when my schedule is busy, I make the time and effort to wake up early and pursue my goals. Having this ritual helps me start my day with a sense of purpose and care for my body, boosting my confidence and overall well-being.

The journey towards better health is unique for everyone, but having a foundation to start from and tailor to your needs can simplify things. Make sure to experiment, listen to your body's responses, and adjust accordingly. Small changes can lead to significant improvements over time.

So, whether you're starting fresh or refining your current routine, approach each morning as an opportunity to set a positive tone for the day ahead, one that honors your body and supports your well-being. With patience, consistency, and self-kindness, you can create a morning ritual that empowers you to thrive, both physically and emotionally.
