What Is PCOS and How I Dealt With It?

5 Actionable Ways I Was Able to Improve My PCOS in a Month

As someone who has struggled with hormonal imbalances and PCOS, I know how frustrating it can be to deal with all the symptoms such as acne, bloating, brain fog, mood swings, etc. Getting a hormone panel is an imperative step in knowing exactly what is going on in your body; however, sometimes it's not feasible, not to mention a Dutch test is $500. That is why the next best thing you could do is the elimination phase. Not a diet, but a phase where you cut out certain groups of food to see if any foods are affecting you negatively or causing inflammation.

Food is supposed to fuel you with energy, not make you groggy or tired. I know it can be hard to cut out certain foods from your diet, and it can even be overwhelming, trust me, I've been there, especially if you live a busy lifestyle and may not have the time and resources to make a fancy meal when you're on the go. This is why I want to make this easy and accessible for you. You just have to be open-minded.

Piece of advice: truly from the bottom of my heart, if you're not willing to change and you are not fully determined to improve your health, then this may not be for you because it takes consistency and determination. You got this!

1. The 2 Week Cut

The 2-week cut. Yes, you read this right. We are going to do a two-week cut! Cutting out certain foods will give you a chance to see how your body reacts to the food you are eating and what might be causing inflammation. I'm sorry to be the bearer of bad news, but it's all in your favor. I noticed that when I did the cut and slowly incorporated back the foods, I was able to see what had made me inflamed and what made me feel good. This was the biggest change in my journey, figuring out what I was intolerant of.

I want you to open a notes app on your phone right now and title it "Foods that I'm cutting out". This will keep you accountable for not eating the foods that you know aren't fueling you. As I am talking from personal experience, cutting out dairy and gluten was a huge help in my acne and bloating. It was the first step to finally figuring out what was causing me so much mental stress due to constant inflammation.

The two-week cut consists of cutting out a wide range of foods and slowly introducing them back into your diet to see if certain foods are reacting badly to your body. For example, ground beef or turkey made me feel super heavy and bloated after eating it. I would not have known this if it wasn't for the cut. It will be tempting to eat outside the cut, but trust me when I say that seeing your mood and body shift from feeling bad to looking and feeling amazing will be enough motivation for you to keep up the good work.

2. Intuitive Eating

Be intentional with what you are consuming. Do not just eat when you are bored, but be aware of when you are hungry or satisfied. Overeating is the biggest cause of weight gain. We tend to overeat when we are bored or stressed. We have to start coming at this from a mental health point of view. Figure out what's causing you stress so you know how to properly deal with it.

Whenever you are bored, instead of being near your kitchen or grabbing a few snacks and watching TV while you eat, go for a walk, listen to a podcast, or start a new hobby! I also know from personal experience that with my hormonal imbalance, I was so hungry all the time! But there's a difference between eating when you're bored and eating when your body is truly telling you it's hungry. Intentional eating is key.

Your body will literally tell you what it needs. Usually, when you crave fruit, it is because your energy is low and you are dehydrated. When you are craving salty savory foods, it's because your body is speaking to you that it needs electrolytes. There's so much more on this, and that's why we have to be in tune with our bodies.

With PCOS, everyone's body is different and reacts differently, but that's why nurturing yourself with proper foods is key. When I started my journey, I did the two-week cut and saw how different foods affected my body. Eating a lot of greens made me less bloated and slimmer, but eating ground beef increased my inflammation. I would not have known this if it wasn't for the cut. Intuitive eating is key!

3. Insulin! Insulin! Insulin!

With PCOS, we do not want to spike our insulin or blood sugar levels. A simple rule I use to maintain no spike is when eating your meals, first eat your veggies, then protein, and finally carbs. This helps you manage your levels without having that spike. Another key tip to this is to make sure you are eating carbs, proteins, and fats within all your meals and snacks to make sure your levels stay stable.

4. GUT Health!

Optimal gut health is crucial. Your gut is where you get rid of metabolic waste and toxins, but how can your gut do this if it's not at its optimal health? Bad gut health can cause fatigue, inflammation, obesity, acne, diabetes, etc. So, some tips to improve poor gut health would be to start taking probiotics. This was life-changing for me, especially since I had been taking antibiotics for my acne for two years, which was just covering a wound with a bandaid.

Fixing my gut did take a while, but taking good probiotics helped me improve my bloating and acne. It also made my bowel movements regularized. When looking for probiotics, I look for ones that have at least 50 billion CFUs. Probiotics at a 50 billion count have a crucial role in maintaining a healthy metabolism. These beneficial bacteria can block fat absorption, reduce the conversion of starch to fat, ease sugar cravings, and lift our moods leading to lower food intake.

Eating fermented foods like kimchi, sauerkraut, ginger, apple cider vinegar, etc., all aided in fixing my gut barrier. Throughout this process, I did cut back from caffeine, just as I noticed that for me, coffee was a bit harsh on my body, especially if you take your coffee with cream and sugar. For coffee and caffeine alternatives, I started drinking green tea, matcha, and London fog, which, by the way, are delicious.

Eating well-cooked vegetables instead of raw vegetables helped in restoring my gut because when you have a poor gut microbiome, you also have less enzyme production to break down and digest raw vegetables, which can often lead to pain, bloating, and gas.

5. My Stress is Stressing Me Out!

Limiting stress. As someone who was in a constant state of fight or flight, when my doctor looked at my hormonal panel, she was stunned. She mentioned to me that she had never seen someone's adrenal levels so poor. I knew I had to do something to fix this. It was causing all my hormones to go out of whack and make matters worse, even though I thought I was eating healthy and working out.

Whenever we feel stressed, our brain's hypothalamus signals our adrenal glands to release and increase levels of stress hormones, including cortisol and adrenaline, or epinephrine. This triggers a fight or flight response, which is our body's way of alarming us that there's an immediate threat we need to address.

The next few weeks, I dedicated myself to prioritizing managing my stress levels. I know this is easier said than done, but if I can do it, so can you. Here are a few things I did to teach myself how to relax:

  • Meditation: Even if it's from 5-10 min in the morning or evening. I encourage you all to look into Joe Dispenza as he was one of the main reasons I started meditating. Anywhere from meditating 2-5x a week will be a good start, and slowly increasing this as you feel comfortable.
  • Grounding: Having direct contact with the earth's ground releases electric charges that stabilize the physiology and helps reduce inflammation, pain, and stress. This also improves blood flow, energy levels, and sleep. Most importantly, it reduces day-night cortisol rhythm, which ultimately reduces stress.
  • Breathwork: This is something that you can do anywhere and anytime. There are many different types of breathwork you can do, including Wim Hof's breathing techniques, physiological sighs, and box breathing. Box breathing is where you inhale for 4 seconds, hold for 4 seconds, exhale for 4 seconds, and hold for 4 seconds again. Repeat this 1-3 times until you feel centered again.
  • Gratitude: Writing down what you are grateful for is always a huge way to reduce stress and can be an incredible source to find happiness in your everyday life. I usually end my day with a prayer and count 5 good things that happened to me, followed by 5 things I am grateful for.

I want to preface that I am not a medical doctor; however, I have been struggling with PCOS for years and have dealt with the repercussions of that. The constant bloating, stress, brain fog, inflammation, and not to mention acne, and mood swings. I have been through this struggle trying to find a way to make me feel normal. I have wasted money on countless doctors who didn't even care to hear me out.

This battle with PCOS seems like a never-ending cycle, but through my journey, I have found ways to holistically change the way I view and deal with PCOS. Now, through hours of researching and meeting with my holistic doctor, I found a way to help my symptoms and get back my period. I aspire to help as many girls as I can by giving them free ways of dealing with the frustrations that come with having PCOS.

Through this blog, I will be sharing all the things that have helped me improve my symptoms and feel better overall. This is a journey and a new lifestyle that I want you all to join me. Remember, you are not alone, and I am here every step of the way giving you all my resources and knowledge.
